Need for Speed Rivals

The game is set in an open world environment called Redview County, where players can choose to race as either a cop or a racer. The story of Need for Speed Rivals is focused on the intense rivalry between police and street racers in Redview County and the adrenaline-fueled racing action that takes place in the game's open world environment.

Need for Speed Payback

The game is set in the fictional Fortune Valley, where players can choose to race as one of three characters and build a crew to take down a criminal cartel known as "The House."
The story of Need for Speed Payback is focused on the characters' desire for revenge against The House and the high-stakes racing action that takes place in the game's open world environment.
In addition to the main storyline, Need for Speed Payback features a large open world environment with various races and events to participate in. The game also includes a variety of cars to unlock and customize, as well as an online multiplayer mode.

How video games are made

Choose a game engine: A game engine is a software framework that provides the tools and features to build games. Some popular game engines include Unity, Unreal Engine, and Godot. Research and select a game engine that aligns with your skill level and development goals. Learn a programming language: Most game engines require you to write code in a programming language. Popular languages used in game development include C#, C++, and Java. Choose a language and start learning its basics. Start small: Begin with a simple game concept and work your way up to more complex projects. This will help you develop your skills gradually and avoid becoming overwhelmed. Build a team: Game development is often a team effort. Consider collaborating with artists, designers, and other developers who can help bring your game to life. Utilize online resources: There are many online resources available to help you learn game development, such as tutorials, forums, and online courses. Take advantage of these resources to enhance your skills and knowledge. Test your game: Regularly test your game throughout the development process to ensure that it works as intended and is fun to play.
